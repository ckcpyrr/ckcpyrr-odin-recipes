Rasmalai

Description

Rasmalai is a traditional Indian dessert made of soft, spongy cheese balls soaked in sweet, flavored milk. The milk is gently reduced and infused with cardamom, saffron, and sometimes rose water, giving it a rich and aromatic taste.

This dessert is light, creamy, and subtly sweet, making it perfect for festive occasions, celebrations, or as a special treat after meals. Its delicate texture and fragrant flavor make it a favorite among all age groups.

Ingredients

  • 10–12 paneer or chenna balls (homemade or store-bought)
  • 4 cups full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar (for milk)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (for paneer balls)
  • 4–5 cardamom pods, crushed
  • A few strands of saffron
  • 1 teaspoon rose water (optional)
  • Chopped pistachios or almonds for garnish

Steps

  1. Boil 4 cups of water with 1/2 cup sugar to make a light syrup. Gently cook the paneer/chenna balls in this syrup for 10–12 minutes. Remove and let them cool.
  2. In a separate pan, bring 4 cups of milk to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer until it thickens slightly.
  3. Add 1/2 cup sugar to the milk, along with crushed cardamom pods and saffron strands. Stir well to dissolve sugar completely.
  4. Gently squeeze excess syrup from the paneer balls and add them to the flavored milk.
  5. Simmer for 5–10 minutes to let the flavors infuse.
  6. Add rose water if using and mix gently.
  7. Remove from heat and let the Rasmalai cool to room temperature, then refrigerate for a few hours.
  8. Garnish with chopped pistachios or almonds before serving.
